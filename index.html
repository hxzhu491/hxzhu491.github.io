About Me

I recently earned my PhD in Biostatistics. My research focuses on statistical machine learning, causal inference, and robust methods for high-dimensional biomedical data, with applications in genomics, single-cell data, and perturbation modeling.

I am particularly interested in developing reliable and interpretable methods for complex biological systems, especially when data are high-dimensional, heterogeneous, or affected by distribution shift.

Research Interests

  • Statistical machine learning
  • Causal inference under hidden confounding
  • High-dimensional biomedical data analysis
  • Single-cell and genomic data science
  • Robust prediction under distribution shift

Selected Work

  • Prediction of perturbation effects on gene expression
  • Biologically relevant evaluation of predictive models
  • Causal inference with hidden confounding and environment shift
